What is a Prescription?

A prescription is a written order from a doctor or any registered medical practitioner to a pharmacist to compound and dispense a specific medicine for a patient.

A prescription acts as a direct link between the physician, pharmacist, and patient.

Different Parts of a Prescription

  • Date
  • Name, Age, Sex and Address of the patient
  • Superscription
  • Inscription
  • Subscription
  • Signature (Sig)
  • Renewal Instruction
  • Signature, Address and Registration number of the prescriber

1. Date

The date is written on the top of the prescription. It helps the pharmacist to know the prescribing date and also helps in renewal.

2. Patient Details

Name and address help in identifying the patient and prevent mixing of prescriptions.

Age and gender help the doctor decide the dose, quantity, and route of administration.

In some cases, patient weight is also required to calculate the correct dose.

4. Inscription

This is the main part of the prescription which contains the name and quantity of the drug.

Parts of Inscription

Base: Active drug that produces therapeutic effect.

Adjuvant: Enhances action or makes preparation more palatable.

Vehicle: Carrier in which drugs dissolve and volume is increased.

5. Subscription

In this part, the prescriber gives directions to the pharmacist regarding dosage form and quantity to be dispensed.

6. Signature (Sig)

Sig gives directions to the patient about drug administration.

  • Quantity to be taken
  • Route of administration
  • Time of administration
  • Special instructions (if any)

7. Renewal Instruction

The prescriber mentions whether the medicine can be renewed or not, and how many times it can be renewed.

This is very important for habit-forming drugs to prevent misuse.

8. Prescriber Details

The prescription must be signed by the doctor. Registration number and address help prevent misuse and illegal dispensing.

Handling of Prescription

  1. Receiving
  2. Reading and checking
  3. Collecting and weighing material
  4. Compounding, labelling and packaging

Sources of Errors in Prescription

  • Abbreviation errors
  • Drug name confusion
  • Strength not mentioned
  • Dosage form confusion
  • Wrong dose
  • Incomplete patient instructions
  • Incompatibilities
